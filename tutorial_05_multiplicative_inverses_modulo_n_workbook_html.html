What is an inverse?

The multiplicative inverse of \(a\) modulo \(n\) is a number \(x\) such that

\(a \cdot x \equiv 1 \pmod{n}\)

We write this as \(x = a^{-1} \pmod{n}\).

Example: \(3^{-1} \equiv 7 \pmod{10}\) because \(3 \cdot 7 = 21 \equiv 1\).

When does it exist?

An inverse exists when \(a\) and \(n\) share no common factor greater than 1.
