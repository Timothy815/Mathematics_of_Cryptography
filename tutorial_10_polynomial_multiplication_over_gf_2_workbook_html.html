Quick Reference — GF(2) Polynomial Multiplication Rules
Multiply powers: add exponents \(x^a \cdot x^b = x^{a+b}\). Example: \(x^3 \cdot x^2 = x^5\).
Multiply by 1 \(x^4 \cdot 1 = x^4\). The constant term 1 has exponent 0, so \(0+4=4\).
Duplicate terms cancel over GF(2) \(x^5 + x^5 = 0\). Coefficients are mod 2, so \(1+1=0\).
Products may exceed degree 7 \(x^7 \cdot x^7 = x^{14}\). Reduction by an irreducible poly comes next (Module 11).
