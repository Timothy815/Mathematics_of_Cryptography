How to use this workbook

Work carefully in layers. First convert bytes to polynomial meaning when needed. Use XOR for addition. Use \(\operatorname{xtime}\) for multiplication by 0x02. If a left shift creates an overflow from the top bit, keep the low byte and XOR with 0x1B.

Quick reference

AES field rules
\[ m(x)=x^8+x^4+x^3+x+1 \] \[ \texttt{0x11B}\longleftrightarrow m(x) \] \[ x^8\equiv x^4+x^3+x+1 \] \[ x^4+x^3+x+1\longleftrightarrow\texttt{0x1B} \]
Useful byte rules
\[ \operatorname{xtime}(a)=a\cdot\texttt{0x02} \] \[ a\cdot\texttt{0x03}=\operatorname{xtime}(a)\oplus a \]

If the top bit was 0, \(\operatorname{xtime}(a)\) is just a left shift. If the top bit was 1, left shift, keep the low byte, then XOR with 0x1B.

Note for self-study

The most common mistake is mixing arithmetic worlds. Stay in the finite-field world: left shifts represent multiplication by \(x\), XOR represents addition, and 0x1B appears when \(x^8\) must be folded down.

Part A: Warm-up vocabulary

A1. \(\mathrm{GF}(2^8)\) is the byte-sized finite-field arithmetic world used by AES. It has 256 possible elements, represented by bytes.

A2. \(\operatorname{xtime}(a)\) means multiply byte \(a\) by \(x\), or by 0x02, inside \(\mathrm{GF}(2^8)\).

A3. 0x11B is the full AES modulus \(x^8+x^4+x^3+x+1\). 0x1B is the lower folded part \(x^4+x^3+x+1\).

A4. It uses polynomial multiplication over \(\mathrm{GF}(2)\), XOR for addition, and reduction by the AES modulus. It is not decimal/integer multiplication.

Part H: Polynomial check

H1 sample answer.

\[ (x^6+x^4+x^2+x+1)(x^4+x+1) \]

The unreduced product is:

\[ x^{10}+x^8+x^7+x^5+x^4+x^3+1 \]

Reducing by \(x^8+x^4+x^3+x+1\) gives:

\[ x^7+x^6+x^5+x^4+x^3+x^2+x \] \[ 11111110_2=\texttt{0xFE} \]

Part I: Concept checks

I1 sample answer. Multiplying by 0x02 means multiplying by \(x\). If the original byte contains \(x^7\), the shift creates \(x^8\). AES folds \(x^8\) into \(x^4+x^3+x+1\), which is 0x1B.

I2 sample answer. 0x03 represents \(x+1\). Multiplying \(a\) by \(x+1\) gives \(xa+a\), which is \(\operatorname{xtime}(a)\oplus a\).

I3 sample answer. The AES modulus provides a folding rule for high powers like \(x^8\), so products that grow beyond byte size are reduced back to degree at most 7.
