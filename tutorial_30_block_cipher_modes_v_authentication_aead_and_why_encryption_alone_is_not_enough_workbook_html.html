Directions

How to use this workbook

Keep the security goals separate. Encryption gives confidentiality. Authentication helps detect tampering and confirms the message verifies under the correct secret key. Modern secure designs usually want both.

Quick reference

MAC formula
\[ tag=MAC_K(message) \]

A keyed tag used to detect modification.

AEAD formula
\[ (ciphertext,tag)=AEAD\_Encrypt_K(nonce,plaintext,AAD) \]

Encryption plus authentication, including optional associated data.

Note for self-study

Part C: MAC and tag practice

C1. \(K\) represents the secret key used to compute the MAC.

C2 sample answer. The receiver recomputes the tag from the received message and the shared key, then compares it to the received tag.

C3 sample answer. The message should be rejected.

C4 sample answer. A MAC is like a keyed tamper seal because changing the message should make the tag fail unless the attacker can compute a valid new tag with the secret key.

Part D: Why encryption alone is not enough

D1. Encryption hides message contents.

D2 sample answer. Encryption alone usually does not prove that the ciphertext was not modified or that it came from someone with the correct key.

D3 sample answer. In CTR, flipping ciphertext bits can flip corresponding plaintext bits after decryption. Without authentication, the receiver may not detect that the ciphertext was changed.

Part I: Concept checks

I1 sample answer. Confidentiality means the content is hidden from unauthorized readers. Integrity means changes to the data can be detected.

I2 sample answer. A message can be hidden but still altered. Encryption may prevent reading, but without authentication the receiver may not know if the ciphertext was modified.

I3 sample answer. AEAD modes are preferred because they provide encryption and authentication together, reducing the chance of combining primitives incorrectly.

I4 sample answer. Encryption alone provides confidentiality, but it does not automatically prove that a message was not changed. A MAC creates a keyed tag that can be verified, and AEAD combines encryption and authentication in one construction. AAD lets visible metadata be authenticated without encrypting it. If tag verification fails, the message should be rejected.
