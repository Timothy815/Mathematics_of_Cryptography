How to use this workbook

This workbook is conceptual. The goal is to understand why AES-GCM-SIV exists, what a synthetic IV is, how AES-GCM-SIV differs from AES-GCM, and why misuse resistance means “more forgiving,” not “mistake proof.”

Quick reference

AES-GCM-SIV high-level idea

        ↓

        ↓

        ↓

        ↓
ciphertext + tag
Careful wording

AES-GCM-SIV is nonce-misuse resistant, not nonce-misuse proof. Nonce reuse is still not recommended.

Note for self-study

AES-GCM-SIV is best understood as a guarded design response to GCM's nonce fragility: authenticate/hash first to create a synthetic IV, then encrypt.

Part B: Review the AES-GCM nonce-reuse problem

StepExpressionMeaning
1\(C_1=P_1\oplus S\)First plaintext encrypted with keystream \(S\).
2\(C_2=P_2\oplus S\)Second plaintext encrypted with the same reused keystream \(S\).
3\(C_1\oplus C_2=P_1\oplus P_2\)The ciphertext XOR reveals a relationship between the plaintexts.
4What happened to \(S\)?\(S\oplus S=0\), so the keystream cancels.

B1 sample answer. AES-GCM-SIV exists partly to reduce this catastrophic nonce-reuse problem. It computes a synthetic IV from the data being protected so repeated nonces do not automatically repeat keystream in the same way.

Part K: Concept checks

K1 sample answer. AES-GCM is fast and effective but strongly depends on external nonce uniqueness. AES-GCM-SIV is more defensive because it computes a synthetic IV from the protected data, reducing the damage from nonce mistakes.

K2 sample answer. If the synthetic IV depends on the message and AAD, then reusing the external nonce does not automatically mean the same encryption starting value is reused for different plaintexts.

K3 sample answer. If exactly the same key, nonce, plaintext, and AAD are used again, the output can repeat. That may reveal that the same protected message was sent or stored again.

K4 sample answer. AES-GCM-SIV is a nonce-misuse-resistant AEAD mode. It is related to AES-GCM but changes the construction by computing a synthetic IV/authentication value before encryption. POLYVAL plays a GHASH-like role in this process. Nonce reuse is still not recommended, but AES-GCM-SIV is designed to fail less catastrophically than AES-GCM when nonce mistakes occur.
