Directions

How to use this workbook

Work through the pipeline as a staged machine. The goal is to understand what each stage contributes: formatting prevents ambiguity, POLYVAL summarizes protected inputs, nonce mixing makes the instance specific, the synthetic IV/tag guides encryption, and verification decides whether the message is accepted.

Quick reference

Synthetic IV pipeline
1. Format AAD, plaintext, and lengths
2. Run POLYVAL over formatted blocks
3. Mix nonce into the POLYVAL result
4. Produce synthetic IV / tag material
5. Use synthetic IV for counter-style encryption
6. During decryption, recompute and verify
POLYVAL accumulator
\[ S_0=0 \] \[ S_i=(S_{i-1}\oplus X_i)\cdot H \]

Note for self-study

Think of AES-GCM-SIV as building the encryption starting point from the protected data itself. POLYVAL helps create the keyed summary, and that summary becomes part of the tag/synthetic IV machinery.

C1 sample answer. Formatting matters because the decrypting side must know exactly what was authenticated. Without clear formatting and lengths, different inputs could potentially be confused.

C2 sample answer. A packet header, sender ID, protocol version, or message number could be AAD.

E1 sample answer. Ordinary AES-GCM relies on the external nonce to set up encryption directly. AES-GCM-SIV mixes the nonce into a synthetic IV construction that also depends on the protected data.

G1 sample answer. \(S\) represents the keystream generated by counter-style encryption.

G2 sample answer. If the encryption setup depends on the synthetic IV, and the synthetic IV depends on protected inputs, then different plaintexts can lead to different keystream setup even when an external nonce repeats.

Part L: Concept checks

L1 sample answer. AES-GCM-SIV formats AAD, plaintext, and length information, then processes those blocks with POLYVAL. The POLYVAL result is combined with nonce information and keyed AES processing to produce synthetic IV/tag material. That synthetic IV is then used to guide counter-style encryption.

L2 sample answer. It is tag-like because it is recomputed and checked for authenticity. It is IV-like because it is used as starting material for counter-style encryption.

L3 sample answer. AES-GCM-SIV reduces nonce-reuse danger because different messages can produce different synthetic IVs even under repeated nonces. But exact repeated inputs can still produce repeated outputs, revealing repetition.

L4 sample answer. AES-GCM-SIV builds a synthetic IV from the protected data before encryption. It formats AAD, plaintext, and lengths, runs POLYVAL to create a keyed summary, mixes in nonce information, produces synthetic IV/tag material, and then encrypts using counter-style encryption. During decryption, the receiver recomputes and verifies the tag. This design reduces nonce-reuse damage but does not make misuse harmless.
