Part F: Misuse resistance reasoning

MistakeWhy it happensWhy misuse resistance helps
Nonce reuseCounters reset, VMs roll back, distributed systems collide.Misuse-resistant modes can reduce catastrophic failure.
Bad API useDevelopers pass values incorrectly or reuse state.Safer APIs can make mistakes harder.
Skipping verificationSystems use decrypted data before checking tags.Clear APIs can refuse plaintext until verification passes.
Manual cryptographyDevelopers assemble primitives incorrectly.High-level AEAD APIs reduce dangerous assembly work.

F1 sample answer. Misuse resistance reduces damage from certain mistakes, but it does not make all incorrect use safe. Some leaks or failures may still occur.

Part K: Mathematical view versus engineering view

ViewMain questionExample
Mathematical/security proof viewWhat is guaranteed if the assumptions hold?AES-GCM is strong when nonce uniqueness holds.

K1 sample answer. A cryptographic mode may be secure under certain assumptions, such as unique nonces. The engineering question is whether the real system can actually preserve those assumptions during crashes, restarts, scaling, updates, and human error.

Part N: Concept checks

N1 sample answer. No AEAD mode is perfect for every situation because systems differ in speed needs, hardware, protocol support, nonce-management reliability, compliance requirements, and likely mistakes.

N2 sample answer. AES-GCM is excellent when nonce uniqueness is maintained because it is fast and widely supported. It becomes dangerous when nonce reuse occurs because confidentiality and authentication can fail badly.

N3 sample answer. Distributed systems may have multiple writers, counter collisions, rollback, and coordination problems. Misuse-resistant AEAD can reduce the damage if nonce management fails.

N4 sample answer. AEAD mode choice involves balancing speed, safety, misuse resistance, and engineering reality. AES-GCM is fast and common but requires strict nonce discipline. AES-GCM-SIV adds guardrails against nonce misuse. ChaCha20-Poly1305-style modes can be attractive on platforms without strong AES acceleration. Real systems should use vetted libraries and APIs that make dangerous mistakes harder.
