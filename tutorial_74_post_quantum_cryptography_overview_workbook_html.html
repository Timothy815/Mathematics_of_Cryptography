How to use this workbook

Note for self-study

The central idea is that post-quantum cryptography replaces vulnerable public-key assumptions with assumptions believed to resist quantum attackers, but migration affects whole systems, not just formulas.

Part A: Warm-up vocabulary

QuestionAnswer
A1. What is post-quantum cryptography?Classical cryptographic algorithms believed secure against both classical and quantum attackers.
A2. What is Shor’s algorithm?A quantum algorithm that efficiently solves factoring and discrete logarithms.
A3. What is Grover’s algorithm?A quantum algorithm that gives a quadratic speedup for unstructured search.
A4. What is a Key Encapsulation Mechanism, or KEM?A method for establishing a shared secret by encapsulating to a public key and decapsulating with a private key.
A5. What is ML-KEM?Module-Lattice-Based Key-Encapsulation Mechanism, used for post-quantum key establishment.
A6. What is ML-DSA?Module-Lattice-Based Digital Signature Algorithm, used for post-quantum signatures.
A7. What is SLH-DSA?Stateless Hash-Based Digital Signature Algorithm, used for post-quantum signatures.
A8. What is a lattice?A repeating grid of points, formally a discrete additive subgroup of a vector space.
A9. What is Learning With Errors, or LWE?A hardness assumption based on solving noisy linear equations.
A10. What is code-based cryptography?Cryptography based on the hardness of decoding random-looking error-correcting codes.
A11. What is a hash-based signature?A digital signature built mainly from hash functions and tree structures.
A12. What is hybrid migration?Combining classical and post-quantum methods during the transition period.
A13. What is crypto-agility?The ability for a system to safely upgrade algorithms, parameters, and protocols.

B1 sample answer. Shor’s algorithm attacks the core mathematical problems behind RSA and elliptic-curve cryptography efficiently on a suitable quantum computer. Grover gives a speedup for search, but symmetric cryptography can often respond by increasing key sizes.

L1 sample answer. Attackers can record encrypted traffic now and wait for future quantum capabilities to break the key exchange. Long-lived secrets may already be exposed later if migration waits too long.

Part P: Concept checks

P1 sample answer. Post-quantum cryptography is classical cryptography designed to remain secure against attackers with quantum computers. It aims to replace or supplement public-key systems such as RSA and elliptic-curve cryptography that are threatened by Shor’s algorithm.

P2 sample answer. Shor’s algorithm threatens factoring and discrete-log-based systems such as RSA, Diffie-Hellman, and elliptic-curve cryptography. Grover’s algorithm gives a quadratic speedup for search, affecting symmetric-key security estimates but usually allowing larger keys as a response.

P3 sample answer. ML-KEM is a lattice-based key encapsulation mechanism for establishing shared secrets. ML-DSA is a lattice-based digital signature algorithm. SLH-DSA is a stateless hash-based digital signature algorithm.

P4 sample answer. Migration affects protocols, certificates, devices, packet sizes, memory, bandwidth, libraries, compliance, and operations. A secure algorithm still has to fit into real systems safely.
