Part 1 — Vocabulary (Q1–5)

Q1. What does the modulus \(n\) represent in modular arithmetic?

Answer: The modulus \(n\) is the wraparound size — the number of positions in the circular number system.

Q2. What does the expression \(a \bmod n\) compute?

Answer: \(a \bmod n\) gives the remainder after dividing \(a\) by \(n\). It satisfies \(a = qn + r\) with \(0 \le r < n\).

Q3. List the elements of \(\mathbb{Z}_7\).

Answer: \(\mathbb{Z}_7 = \{0, 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6\}\).

Q4. State the two equivalent tests for \(a \equiv b \pmod{n}\).

Answer: (i) \(a\) and \(b\) have the same remainder modulo \(n\). (ii) \(n\) divides \(a - b\).

Q5. Why are the standard residues modulo \(n\) labelled \(0\) through \(n-1\) rather than \(1\) through \(n\)?

Answer: There are exactly \(n\) residues. Starting at 0 and going to \(n-1\) gives \(n\) positions, and \(n\) itself wraps back to 0.
