Part 1 — Vocabulary (Q1–5)  Written responses — not auto-checked

Q1. What does the modulus \(n\) represent in modular arithmetic?

The modulus \(n\) is the wraparound size — the count of positions in the circular number system before returning to 0.

Q2. What does the expression \(a \bmod n\) compute?

\(a \bmod n\) gives the remainder \(r\) after dividing \(a\) by \(n\), satisfying \(a = qn + r\) with \(0 \le r < n\).

Q3. List the elements of \(\mathbb{Z}_8\).

\(\mathbb{Z}_8 = \{0, 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 7\}\)

Q4. State the two equivalent tests for \(a \equiv b \pmod{n}\).

(i) \(a\) and \(b\) have the same remainder modulo \(n\). (ii) \(n\) divides \(a - b\).

Q5. Explain in one or two sentences why negative numbers can have a well-defined result modulo \(n\).

Modular arithmetic defines residues as the unique value in \(\{0,\ldots,n-1\}\) that is congruent to the number. Adding \(n\) (as many times as needed) brings any negative integer into that range.
