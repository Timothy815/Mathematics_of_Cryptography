GF(2) Polynomial Multiplication Rules

Step 1 — Multiply single terms

Add the exponents: \(x^i \cdot x^j = x^{i+j}\)

Examples:

  • \(x^3 \cdot x^4 = x^7\)
  • \(x^6 \cdot x^2 = x^8\)
  • \(x^5 \cdot 1 = x^5\)

Step 2 — Distribute

Multiply every term of one polynomial by every term of the other (like FOIL, but for any number of terms).

Step 3 — Cancel like terms (GF(2))

Over GF(2), \(1+1=0\). Any term that appears an even number of times cancels; odd occurrences survive.

Result = unreduced product

The result may have powers above \(x^7\). This is called the unreduced product. Reduction modulo an irreducible polynomial is covered in Module 11.

Example:
\((x^2+1)(x+1)\)
\(= x^3 + x^2 + x + 1\)
(all terms distinct, none cancel)

Self-study note

For every multiplication: (1) multiply each pair of terms by adding exponents; (2) collect like terms; (3) cancel any term appearing an even number of times — those coefficients equal \(1+1+\cdots=0\) mod 2. The result is the unreduced product; powers above \(x^7\) are valid here.

G1. False. \(3+5=8\), so \(x^3 \cdot x^5 = x^8\), not \(x^{15}\).

G2. True. \(x^4+x^4 = 0\) because \(1+1=0\) mod 2.

Part H: Written Explanation (Sample Answers)

H1 sample. Addition over GF(2) corresponds to XOR on bits: each of the 8 bit positions is handled independently, and the result is still within the same 8-bit range. Multiplication, by contrast, involves adding exponents. When two high-degree terms are multiplied — for example \(x^7 \cdot x^7 = x^{14}\) — the resulting degree exceeds 7. Addition cannot increase the degree because it only combines existing terms; it never creates a new, higher-degree term.

H2 sample. Both forms of polynomial multiplication use the same distributive rule (multiply every term of one polynomial by every term of the other) and the same rule for single terms (\(x^i \cdot x^j = x^{i+j}\)). The key difference is how like terms are combined: over the integers, coefficients are added normally (so \(x^3 + x^3 = 2x^3\)), whereas over GF(2) all arithmetic is mod 2, meaning \(x^3 + x^3 = 0\). This cancellation of duplicates is the only change; the distribution step is identical.
